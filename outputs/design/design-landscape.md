# CadVerify — Design-Landscape Research: Reference-Class Patterns (Adopt / Avoid)

**Role:** Design-landscape researcher (research only — no UI built here).
**Goal:** Study how the reference class designs this category, extract *specific, sourced* patterns, and give each one an **adopt** or **avoid** call tied to CadVerify's glass-box thesis. This is the pattern library the design/build harness pulls from.
**Date:** 2026-06-29.
**Companion docs (do not duplicate):** `competitor-ux.md` (category/IA map, per-product UX), `design-direction.md` (locked direction), `design-system-patterns.md`, `design-system-spec.md`. This file is the *annotated pattern lineage + the 2026 bars*, not a re-survey.

---

## 0. How to read this

- The reference class splits into **three design lineages**, each with distinct DNA. CadVerify must *borrow the surface polish of the first, reject the ceremony of the second, and adopt the craft discipline of the third* — while doing the one thing none of them do: **make the cost model itself the editable, cited UI** (the glass box).
- Every pattern below carries: **what it is (sourced)** → **ADOPT / AVOID / ADOPT-WITH-INVERSION** → **why, for our thesis**.
- The thesis spine each call serves: **(1) glass-box is the hero**, **(2) the decision not the dollar**, **(3) role-aware**, **(4) 2026 = clarity/density/speed/trust, not flash.**
- Sourcing honesty: these products gate real pixels behind login/demo. Where a UI detail is *stated* in a cited help-center / product page it is quoted; where *inferred* from feature copy it is tagged `[inferred]`. No screenshots were fabricated.

### The three lineages at a glance

| Lineage | Exemplars | Design DNA | Relationship to CadVerify |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A. Instant-quote DFM marketplace** | Xometry, Paperless Parts, Protolabs (ProDesk), Fictiv | Upload → configure → live re-quote → checkout. Consumer-grade funnel polish, 3D-viewer + DFM badges, fast. | **Borrow the surface + the loop.** Their funnel is excellent; their cost logic is hidden (it has to be — they're selling *their* capacity). We expose the logic they hide. |
| **B. Cost-engineer heavyweight** | aPriori (core), Siemens Teamcenter PCM | Bottom-up bill-of-process, spreadsheet-grade tables, role dashboards, physics-based + defensible — but dense, training-heavy, intimidating-on-open. | **Invert it.** Keep the defensibility + provenance; reject the density-as-default and the training tax. This is the black box we attack. |
| **C. Modern technical-B2B craft** | Linear, Vercel/Geist | Density-done-well, keyboard-first, optimistic UI, Swiss clarity, mono for numbers, evidence-over-taste, AI-native. | **Adopt the discipline wholesale.** This is the 2026 quality bar that makes a type-A tool *read* as trustworthy as a type-B platform without inheriting B's weight. |

## 1. The instant-quote DFM lineage (Lineage A) — borrow the loop, expose what they hide

This is the closest reference class to CadVerify's front door. The category has converged on a remarkably consistent interaction grammar; the patterns below are the load-bearing ones.

### 1.1 The live re-quote loop — "pricing updates instantly as you make selections"
**What it is (sourced).** Xometry: after upload, the user opens **"Edit Configuration"** and sets process, material, finish, quantity, inspection; *"Pricing and lead times will update instantly as you make selections."* ([xometry.com/how-xometry-works](https://www.xometry.com/how-xometry-works/)). Protolabs ProDesk: *"pricing and lead times updating in real time"* as the user changes material variants, secondary processing, finishes ([manufactur3dmag](https://manufactur3dmag.com/protolabs-prodesk-ai-dfm-real-time-quoting/), [tctmagazine](https://www.tctmagazine.com/protolabs-lau/)). Xometry CAD add-in: *"quotes updating instantly with each change."*
**Call: ADOPT — and make the lever the decision, not the dollar.** The instant tweak→rerun loop is the single most important interaction CadVerify inherits. **But** the comps' lever flips a *price*; ours flips a **decision**. The signature interaction (per `design-direction.md`) is a **quantity slider that live-flips the recommended process at the make-vs-buy crossover** — same loop, but the output that moves is "CNC ≤ 740 units → mold wins above 740," not just $/unit. This is the loop the comps nail mechanically and miss strategically. The engine already emits the crossover (`report_to_dict` → make-vs-buy crossover); we surface it on the slider.

### 1.2 DFM as a badge on the viewer, two-way-linked to the issue
**What it is (sourced).** Fictiv is the cleanest spec: a **yellow badge on the CAD preview** + a **yellow warning above the quote**; a **"View feedback"** button opens the list; and for many issue types *"click on the DFM message in order to highlight the areas of the part that the DFM is referring to"* ([fictiv.com/help](https://www.fictiv.com/help/getting-a-quote/how-do-i-view-and-respond-to-dfm-feedback)). Protolabs ProDesk: *"an interactive three-dimensional image… allows you to review part geometry and assess potential design issues that are highlighted"* ([protolabs.com](https://www.protolabs.com/automated-design-analysis/)). Xometry surfaces this in a dedicated **"Analyze" tab** of automated DFM checks ([xometry how-it-works](https://www.xometry.com/how-xometry-works/)).
**Call: ADOPT the interaction, INVERT the severity model.** The viewer↔issue two-way link (click message → geometry highlights) is the canonical DFM interaction and we should match it pixel-for-pixel in capability. **But** the comps stop at "here's a risk, talk to us." Our thesis upgrade: **named issues split into Required-fix vs Advisory** (not opaque numeric scores — `design-direction.md`), and each issue is **actionable, not a red flag** (see §4.1) — it states the threshold it failed and the value that would pass. Yellow-badge-then-go-chat-an-expert is a marketplace tactic to pull you into their sales loop; our glass box answers the "why" in-product.

### 1.3 Color-coded severity as the universal status language
**What it is (sourced).** The whole lineage uses traffic-light semantics: Fictiv's yellow badge/warning; the category convention of green-OK / amber-advisory / red-blocking on DFM results. CadVerify already single-sources this (`frontend/src/lib/status.ts`; green pass / amber advisory / red required-fix per `design-direction.md`).
**Call: ADOPT — already done, hold the line.** Strict, single-sourced semantic status is table stakes and a trust signal. The discipline to *avoid* is decorative color: an aerospace/AV buyer reads color as data. Reserve hue for status + the one steel-blue accent; never for ornament.

### 1.4 The role handoff — "Forward to Purchaser"
**What it is (sourced).** Xometry ships an explicit **"Forward to Purchaser"** button to *"share your quote with a purchaser or team member"* before checkout ([xometry how-it-works](https://www.xometry.com/how-xometry-works/)). Protolabs ProDesk: quotes, orders, and the **Production Catalog** *"can be shared with colleagues"* ([metal-am](https://www.metal-am.com/protolabs-unveils-prodesk-platform-for-prototyping-and-production/)). The broader CPQ category treats the quote→order handoff (engineering → purchasing → sourcing) as a first-class workflow with auto-notification of each function ([renaissancetech enterprise quoting](https://renaissancetech.com/enterprise-quoting/)).
**Call: ADOPT — this *is* the role-aware thesis made literal.** Our pillar (3) is "same engine, different views: design engineer / cost engineer / sourcing / manufacturing engineer." The handoff button is how those roles connect without four logins. CadVerify's version is sharper than "forward to purchaser": **share the glass box, not the number** — the recipient lands on the same provenance-tagged, editable cost model with confidence intervals, so "your numbers become yours" survives the handoff. This is a concrete API/build gap to flag: shared/role-scoped views of one analysis object.

### 1.5 Forward-the-RFQ-email front door + spec auto-extraction (Paperless Parts)
**What it is (sourced).** Paperless Parts' **Wingman™** lets a shop *"forward RFQ emails and all attachments directly into Paperless Parts"* and auto-extracts the package — it *"recognizes over 10,000 ASTM, AMS, MIL-SPEC, NADCAP, GD&T, and OEM-specific process/material specifications"* and pulls threads, part numbers, material, and 3D models ([paperlessparts quote-workflow](https://www.paperlessparts.com/quote-workflow-management/)). Result: single component quoted in ~15 min vs a ~2-hour manual process. ITAR / CMMC Level 2 compliant.
**Call: ADOPT the zero-friction ingestion, ADOPT the compliance posture; this is AI-native done right.** Two lessons. (a) **Meet the work where it lives** — the cold upload form is friction; ingestion-by-forwarding/CAD-plugin is the 2026 bar (see §4.4). (b) **Spec recognition is a glass-box opportunity**: when the engine reads a GD&T callout or material spec, it should *show what it read and tag the provenance* (MEASURED-from-geometry vs USER-from-drawing vs DEFAULT) — exactly the provenance model the cost engine already emits. The ITAR/CMMC framing is non-negotiable for Aramco/Zoox and should be a visible trust signal, not buried.

### 1.6 The "engineered, not estimated" / deterministic trust claim
**What it is (sourced).** The lineage sells *defensibility of the number*: 3D Spark markets *"engineered, not estimated"* and *"deterministic"* costing (`competitor-ux.md`); aPriori sells *"physics-based, deterministic… not historical cost data or supplier-provided numbers"* ([apriori aiSource](https://www.apriori.com/solutions/products/aisource/)). Xometry, by contrast, leans ML/statistical — a *"conversion rate model… price-response function for every individual quote"* trained on *"millions of part files"* ([globenewswire/Xometry](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/03/03/3248487/0/en/xometry-deepens-ai-native-marketplace-advantage-with-new-enterprise-lead-time-intelligence-and-personalized-pricing-models.html)).
**Call: ADOPT the deterministic framing, but make it *visible*, not just claimed.** This is the crux of the wedge. Both camps *assert* trust; neither *shows* it. Xometry's ML price is the ultimate black box (you cannot audit a learned price-response function). aPriori's physics is auditable in principle but buried under ceremony (§2). **CadVerify's claim is stronger because it's demonstrated, not asserted:** every driver is on screen, provenance-tagged MEASURED/USER/DEFAULT/SHOP with a source string, summing to the unit cost, with a confidence interval. Marketing must say *"validated on your parts"* (per-shop calibration on held-out real parts) — **never** a fabricated ±X% (none measured yet; pending first real-data/Zoox session). The method *is* the trust signal.

### 1.7 Hybrid automated + human-expert DFM (Fictiv) — a tell about coverage
**What it is (sourced).** Fictiv blends *"instant automated warnings"* with *"customized recommendations from manufacturing engineers as needed"* and live annotations from real engineers in the 3D viewer; deeper feedback arrives within ~2 business days ([fictiv help](https://www.fictiv.com/help/getting-a-quote/how-do-i-view-and-respond-to-dfm-feedback), [fictiv live annotations](https://www.fictiv.com/articles/manufacturing-feedback-from-real-engineers-introducing-live-annotations-on-the-fictiv-platform)).
**Call: ADOPT-WITH-CAUTION — humans-in-the-loop is honest; the 2-day latency is the gap we beat.** The lesson is *epistemic honesty*: Fictiv admits the automation doesn't cover everything and routes to a human. CadVerify should be equally honest — that's what **confidence intervals + provenance tags are for** (DEFAULT-tagged drivers are the "we're guessing here" signal). But the human-loop's 2-day latency is exactly the friction our instant glass box removes. Inherit the honesty (show confidence, show what's a default), reject the latency (don't make trust depend on an async human).

## 2. aPriori (Lineage B) — the black box we attack, drawn concretely

aPriori is the most important *anti-pattern*: it is the incumbent the thesis names as the opaque, training-heavy black box. The contrast below is the wedge, made concrete.

### 2.1 Density-as-default and "intimidating on open"
**What it is (sourced).** Reviewers: *"The complexity of what you see when you open the tool can be intimidating to users that do not use it often"*; *"complex usability and difficult learning"*; *"complex parameters and occasional interface quirks"* ([G2/aPriori](https://www.g2.com/products/apriori-digital-manufacturing-simulation-software/reviews), [softwareconnect](https://softwareconnect.com/reviews/apriori-software/)). Even aPriori concedes the fix is to push *"base level functionality… into CAD programs… [for] a less complex environment."*
**Call: AVOID — this is the exact failure mode the thesis exists to beat.** aPriori puts the **bill-of-process spreadsheet first and the answer nowhere** — the user must *be* a cost engineer to extract the decision. CadVerify inverts: **answer-first, glass-box-on-demand** (`design-direction.md` north star). The density isn't wrong *per se* (cost engineers want it — §3.1); putting it in the default view *is*. Concrete rule: the design engineer's landing is the decision (process + crossover + confidence); the bill-of-process is one tab/click away for the cost engineer who wants it. Same engine, role-gated density.

### 2.2 Training-heavy + exponential complexity
**What it is (sourced).** *"The time complexity is a bit of an issue — as the number of items increases, the number of possible itemsets grows exponentially"*; mixed/steep learning curve requiring fundamentals-building ([G2/aPriori](https://www.g2.com/products/apriori-digital-manufacturing-simulation-software/reviews), [capterra/aPriori](https://www.capterra.com/p/162255/aPriori/)).
**Call: AVOID the training tax; ADOPT zero-onboarding.** A tool that needs a certification to extract a number cannot serve the design engineer's *"fast, glanceable, tweak-rerun"* loop (thesis pillar 3). The 2026 bar is *no cold-start, no manual* — drop a file, get the decision. The depth is reachable, never required.

### 2.3 The auditability paradox — and aPriori's own 2026 admission
**What it is (sourced).** aPriori's genuine strength is defensible, physics-based, *"transparent cost logic"* for *"fact-based"* supplier negotiation ([apriori should-cost](https://www.apriori.com/should-cost-analysis/)). Yet its 2026 move, **aiSource**, tacitly admits the black-box problem: *"aPriori has been generating objective cost data for over two decades… **the new piece is that AI lets a non-expert use it in a live conversation**"* — an AI assistant that *"translates should-cost… into clear negotiation strategies so every team member can engage"* ([businesswire/aiSource](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260526130779/en/aPriori-Launches-aiSource-an-AI-Sourcing-Solution-Giving-Procurement-Teams-the-Manufacturing-and-Cost-Intelligence-to-Win-More-Supplier-Negotiations), [apriori aiSource](https://www.apriori.com/solutions/products/aisource/)).
**Call: ADOPT the defensibility, AVOID the "wrap a chatbot around the black box" fix — beat it with native glass-box.** This is the most important competitive signal in the landscape: **the category leader is bolting an AI translator onto a tool too opaque for non-experts to use.** That's a patch on the wrong layer. CadVerify's answer is structurally better: don't *translate* the black box after the fact — **make the model legible in the first place** (every driver visible, sourced, editable). When the model is already glass, the AI layer (§4.3) explains and acts on a substrate the user can already see and verify, instead of being the *only* way in. Our line: *they need an AI to read you their black box; ours is glass — and the AI shows its work against it.*

### 2.4 Per-shop / regional calibration — adopt the substance, not the gating
**What it is (sourced).** aPriori's defensibility rests on *"configurable digital factories… real-world Regional Data Libraries (RDLs) and physics-based manufacturing process models"* ([apriori PCM glossary](https://www.apriori.com/glossary/product-cost-management-software/)).
**Call: ADOPT the substance — CadVerify already has it, and exposes it better.** Regional/shop calibration is correct and credible; it's why "same part costs differently per shop." CadVerify's engine already binds real shop rates (`backend/data/shop_profiles/`, every rate tagged + sourced). The differentiator is *exposure*: aPriori's RDLs live inside an admin/cost-engineer surface; CadVerify should surface **"calibrated to Midwest Precision CNC — here are their rates and where each came from"** inline in the glass box, visible to the design engineer, not locked behind a config role. (API/build gap: shop calibration + confidence + routing live in the engine but aren't fully exposed in API/frontend yet — flag for build harness.)

## 3. Modern technical-B2B craft (Lineage C) — adopt the discipline wholesale

This lineage is *how* you make a data-dense, type-A tool read as trustworthy and 2026-modern without consumer flash. It is the quality bar.

### 3.1 Linear: density-done-well + "every element earns its position"
**What it is (sourced).** Linear's interface is *"denser than what most design teams ship, but every visible element earns its position through ruthless prioritization of what engineering teams do daily"*; *"information density and clarity coexist because the design team holds strong opinions about workflow."* It applies a **4/8px grid religiously** (inconsistent spacing is *"the fastest way to make a clean design feel cheap"*), uses **muted colors + progressive disclosure**, and is **opinionated** over configurable ([925studios Linear breakdown](https://www.925studios.co/blog/linear-design-breakdown-saas-ui-2026), [logrocket](https://blog.logrocket.com/ux-design/linear-design/)).
**Call: ADOPT — this resolves the central tension of the build.** CadVerify must serve *opposing* density needs (glanceable design engineer vs traceable cost engineer) without drowning either. Linear's resolution is the template: **high density is fine if every element earns its place and spacing is religious.** Concrete adopts: the 8px grid is already locked (`design-direction.md`); add a **density toggle on the big cost-driver tables** (cost engineer wants compact, right-aligned-numeric, frozen-ID rows); hold "opinionated defaults over configuration" (don't ship 40 toggles — ship the right view per role).

### 3.2 Linear: speed *is* a feature — optimistic UI, skeletons, caching
**What it is (sourced).** *"Every interaction feels instant because of optimistic UI, skeleton states, and aggressive caching"*; the keyboard-first/command-palette model works because the audience *"already thinks in shortcuts"* ([925studios](https://www.925studios.co/blog/linear-design-breakdown-saas-ui-2026)).
**Call: ADOPT — speed is a trust signal for this audience.** The tweak→rerun loop (§1.1) only feels like a *conversation with the model* if it's instant. Adopt optimistic UI + skeletons for re-quote, and a **command palette** (engineers think in shortcuts — switch part, change qty, jump to a driver, export). Slowness reads as "it's grinding/guessing"; instant reads as "it knows."

### 3.3 Vercel/Geist: Swiss clarity + mono-for-numbers
**What it is (sourced).** Geist: *"simplicity, minimalism, speed… precision, clarity, and functionality at its core,"* Swiss-design lineage; **Geist Mono for headings/technical type + Geist Sans for body**; text almost always **#171717 or #4d4d4d on white**; pixel-level glyph craft ([designsystems.surf/vercel](https://designsystems.surf/design-systems/vercel), [basement.studio Geist](https://basement.studio/post/the-birth-of-geist-a-typeface-crafted-for-the-web)).
**Call: ADOPT — and we already do.** This validates the locked direction: clean UI sans + **monospace for every number** (cost, dimensions, lead time, qty), tabular figures, restrained near-black text on light (plus dark mode). The lesson is *numbers are the product, so typeset them like instruments* — mono + tabular makes a cost table scan like a gauge cluster, which is exactly the "instrument-grade confidence" the aerospace/AV buyer trusts. Reject gradient/glassmorphism flash — Swiss restraint *is* the credibility.

### 3.4 Vercel: "evidence, not taste" + "design every reachable state" (AI-native craft rules)
**What it is (sourced).** Vercel's published rules for teaching *agents* to design product (directly applicable to an AI-native build): **"Start with the job, not the pixels"** (identify user, goal, object, system change first); **"Use evidence, not taste"** (decisions trace to product behavior / repo guidance / verified adjacent patterns); **"Treat shipped code as evidence, not automatic precedent"**; **"Design every reachable state"** (not just the happy path); **"Name the exact object, scope, and consequence of important actions — Verb + Noun, never Confirm/OK/a bare verb"**; **"Prefer inline disclosure before adding a modal"**; **"Prefer strong defaults and direct behavior over adding configuration"**; **"Preserve user input through validation and recoverable errors."** Deterministic checks (nested-modal, icon-button a11y, radio-vs-select for 2–3 options) are linter-enforced; judgment calls stay with the human/agent ([vercel.com/blog/teaching-agents-product-design-at-vercel](https://vercel.com/blog/teaching-agents-product-design-at-vercel)).
**Call: ADOPT as the design-spec contract for the whole program.** Two reasons this is gold. (a) It is the **Validation-Auditor's rubric pre-written**: "evidence not taste" = our "every decision traces to a real audience need or studied pattern." Use these verbatim as acceptance criteria. (b) Concrete UI adopts for the glass box: **"design every reachable state"** → the cost screen must design the low-confidence / missing-data / DEFAULT-heavy / failed-routing states, not just the clean answer (a confident wrong answer is the trust-killer for this buyer); **"Verb + Noun, never Confirm/OK"** → buttons like *"Override material rate"*, *"Recalculate for 5,000 units"*, *"Share glass box with purchaser"*; **"inline disclosure before modal"** → the provenance/"why this number" expands inline in the table, it does not pop a dialog (the glass box should never be *behind* a door).

## 4. The 2026 bars — the four non-negotiables, and how each serves the glass box

These are the category-wide expectations as of 2026. Missing any one reads as "2021 tool." Each is sourced and tied to the thesis.

### 4.1 BAR: Actionable, not red-flags
**What it is (sourced).** The bar: *"actionable feedback, not just red flags — telling an engineer the minimum wall thickness for their selected material and process and suggesting a specific dimension that would work is far more useful than a generic warning."* Pain point named explicitly: *"non-actionable outputs — lengthy reports without clear, practical recommendations that waste engineering time."* And **context-dependence**: *"a 0.5mm wall might be fine for a stamped stainless steel bracket but catastrophic for a glass-filled nylon injection-molded part"* ([getleo.ai DFM 2026](https://www.getleo.ai/blog/dfm-analysis-tools-instant-feedback-2026), [fictiv DFM guide](https://www.fictiv.com/articles/dfm-design-for-manufacturing-guide)).
**Call: ADOPT — and it's a direct extension of glass-box.** A glass-box issue is *inherently* actionable because the threshold and the measured value are both on screen: not "wall too thin (red)" but **"wall 0.6 mm < 1.0 mm min for this process/material [MEASURED 0.6 / DEFAULT-threshold 1.0]; widen to ≥1.0 mm or switch process."** Context-dependence maps onto per-shop calibration (a shop's real min wall, tagged SHOP). This bar and our thesis are the same idea: *show the number, the rule, and the fix.*

### 4.2 BAR: Verifiable / cited reasoning
**What it is (sourced).** 2026 enterprise standard: *"transparency as evidence, not reassurance"*; **system-level citations** that *"map claims back to specific sources with independent attribution"*; UX requirement of **inline citations (not appended URL lists)**, **click-through to the exact source passage**, and *"passage-level citation is more useful than document-level"* ([seekr citation agents](https://www.seekr.com/blog/how-citation-based-agents-build-trust/), [ust AI interpretability 2026](https://www.ust.com/en/insights/ai-interpretability-explainability-2026-executive-view)).
**Call: ADOPT — this is literally the glass-box spec in another vocabulary.** "Inline, passage-level, click-through to source" = our **per-driver provenance tag (MEASURED/USER/DEFAULT/SHOP) + source string, expandable inline** (§3.4). The engine already emits the source string per driver; the UI must render it as a *clickable, inline citation on the number itself*, not a footnote. "Transparency as evidence not reassurance" is the marketing line: we don't *ask* for trust, we *show the receipts* — and confidence intervals are the honesty layer (don't over-claim a DEFAULT-heavy estimate).

### 4.3 BAR: AI-native (and MCP-ready)
**What it is (sourced).** Every serious player went AI-native in 2025–26: Xometry's ML conversion-rate pricing trained on *"millions of part files"* ([globenewswire/Xometry](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/03/03/3248487/0/en/xometry-deepens-ai-native-marketplace-advantage-with-new-enterprise-lead-time-intelligence-and-personalized-pricing-models.html)); Protolabs ProDesk *"AI-driven DFM"* ([3dadept](https://3dadept.com/protolabs-prodesk-helps-manufacturers-tap-into-ai-quoting-and-improved-production-management/)); Paperless Parts Wingman; aPriori aiSource conversational sourcing. Infra: **MCP is the 2026 standard** — *"the USB-C for AI,"* 97M monthly SDK downloads, adopted by Anthropic/OpenAI/Google/Microsoft/Amazon, 10,000+ public servers; Cursor/Claude Code/Continue plug in any MCP server ([n1n.ai MCP 2026](https://explore.n1n.ai/blog/mcp-tools-2026-model-context-protocol-guide-2026-05-12)).
**Call: ADOPT — but our AI is glass-box-native, which is the differentiation.** The trap (aPriori, §2.3): bolt a chatbot onto an opaque model so it's the *only* way in. Our inversion: **AI explains and acts on a model the user can already see.** Concrete posture: (a) an assistant that answers *"why is this $44/unit?"* by pointing at the on-screen drivers (cited, not generated-from-nowhere) and can *"re-run at 5,000 units"* or *"override the material rate"* — verifiable because the substrate is glass; (b) **expose CadVerify's cost engine as an MCP server** so the model is callable from the engineer's own agent/IDE/CAD-adjacent workflow (build-harness/API roadmap item — the engine's `report_to_dict` is the natural MCP payload). AI-native for *this* audience means **auditable AI**, not a magic price.

### 4.4 BAR: Workflow-integrated (meet the work where it lives)
**What it is (sourced).** The cold web form is dying. Xometry ships **CAD add-ins** that put live pricing/DFM *inside* SOLIDWORKS, Autodesk Fusion, and Onshape ([xometry CAD add-ins](https://www.xometry.com/cad-add-ins/)) and partnered with **Siemens** (+$50M) to embed instant quoting/DFM into the Designcenter design tool ([engineering.com](https://www.engineering.com/siemens-and-xometry-to-bring-instant-quoting-dfm-analysis-to-designcenter/)). Paperless Parts ingests by **forwarded RFQ email**. CPQ category: ERP/PLM sync + one-click quote→order with auto-notify of engineering/purchasing/sourcing ([renaissancetech](https://renaissancetech.com/enterprise-quoting/)).
**Call: ADOPT as roadmap — web app first, but design for embedding.** The full glass box lives in the web app (where the editable cost model + crossover + 3D viewer have room), but the *trigger* and the *handoff* must reach into the engineer's tools: a CAD/PLM hook to send a part to CadVerify, the forward-an-RFQ ingestion path (§1.5), and the role handoff (§1.4) that notifies sourcing/manufacturing. For Zoox/Aramco specifically, **PLM/ITAR-aware integration is a buying requirement, not a nicety** (Paperless's ITAR/CMMC posture is the bar). Flag for build harness: the API must support an embeddable/headless quote trigger + a shareable analysis object, not only the interactive page.

## 5. Synthesis — the glass-box differentiation, in one table

Where each lineage stops, and where CadVerify's thesis takes over:

| Dimension | Lineage A (Xometry/Fictiv/ProDesk) | Lineage B (aPriori) | **CadVerify glass-box move** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The number** | ML/marketplace price — unauditable by design (selling *their* capacity) | Physics-based but buried in bill-of-process | Every driver visible, **provenance-tagged + sourced**, sums to unit cost, with **confidence interval** |
| **The hero output** | A price + lead time | A cost spreadsheet | **The decision** — process choice + make-vs-buy crossover (qty slider live-flips it) |
| **DFM** | Risk badge → "chat an expert" (2-day loop) | Deep checks, expert-only surface | **Actionable, named** Required vs Advisory; states failed threshold + the value that passes |
| **Default density** | Low (funnel) | Very high (intimidating-on-open) | **Role-aware**: answer-first for design eng; depth one tab away for cost eng (density toggle) |
| **Trust mechanism** | *Asserted* ("engineered not estimated") | *Asserted + role-gated* | **Demonstrated** — "validated on your parts," receipts inline (NEVER a fabricated ±X%) |
| **AI** | Magic price / chatbot | Chatbot wrapping the black box | **Glass-box-native** — AI explains/acts on a model you can already see; engine as MCP server |
| **Calibration** | Hidden (it's their shop) | RDLs in admin surface | **Per-shop, surfaced inline** — "calibrated to <shop>, here's each rate's source" |

### Top adopts (do these)
1. **The live tweak→rerun loop (§1.1)** — but the lever flips the *decision* (crossover), not just the price.
2. **Viewer↔issue two-way DFM link (§1.2)**, upgraded to actionable Required/Advisory (§4.1).
3. **Role handoff = share the glass box, not the number (§1.4)** — the role-aware thesis made literal.
4. **Provenance tags rendered as inline, click-through citations (§4.2)** — receipts on the number.
5. **Linear's density-done-well + speed (§3.1–3.2)**; **Geist's mono-for-numbers + Swiss restraint (§3.3)**.
6. **Vercel's "evidence not taste" + "design every reachable state" + "Verb+Noun" + "inline before modal" (§3.4)** as the build/audit contract — *design the low-confidence states*.
7. **AI-native = auditable AI + engine-as-MCP-server (§4.3)**; ingestion/embedding into CAD/PLM/email (§4.4).

### Top avoids (don't do these)
1. **aPriori's density-as-default + training tax (§2.1–2.2)** — answer-first or bust.
2. **The "chatbot over a black box" fix (§2.3)** — make the model legible, don't translate the opacity.
3. **Asserted-not-shown trust + any fabricated accuracy figure** — show receipts; "validated on your parts."
4. **Marketplace DFM's "badge → go chat an expert" 2-day loop (§1.7)** — answer the "why" in-product, instantly; use confidence to be honest, not a human queue.
5. **Decorative color / consumer flash (§1.3, §3.3)** — for an aerospace/AV buyer, flash *reduces* trust; color is data.

## 6. Open build/API gaps flagged (owned by build harness, not design)
The engine already emits what the glass box needs; the API/frontend don't fully expose it yet:
- **Confidence intervals, geometric routing (archetype + recommended process + reasoning), and per-shop calibration** live in `report_to_dict` but aren't surfaced through `/api/v1/validate/cost` → frontend. Design assumes them; build must expose them.
- **Shareable / role-scoped analysis object** for the handoff (§1.4) and embed (§4.4) — currently a single interactive page, not a shareable object.
- **Engine-as-MCP-server** (§4.3) — `report_to_dict` is the natural payload; not yet exposed.
- **Embeddable/headless quote trigger** (CAD/PLM/forward-RFQ ingestion, §4.4) — not yet present.
