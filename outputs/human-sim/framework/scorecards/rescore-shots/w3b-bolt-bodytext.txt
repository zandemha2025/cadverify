HONEST BOUNDARIES · FACT vs ASSUMPTION vs GATE

Quantity — FACT

REAL FACT: the per-part quantity is the true instance count of that design in THIS assembly, counted from the extracted product tree. It is fed to the cost engine as the real per-assembly volume signal.

Annual volume — USER-DECLARED

USER-DECLARED: annual volume = per-assembly quantity × assemblies_per_year. assemblies_per_year is not in the geometry; unset => cost is reported at the per-assembly quantity only (the honest FACT), never an invented annual figure.

Material — DEFAULT assumption

DEFAULT ASSUMPTION: AP203/AP214 geometry carries no material. The costed material_class is a stated default (overridable), not derived from the part. A per-part material inference is a future labelled tier.

Interference — REAL geometry, not a verdict

REAL GEOMETRY, NOT A VERDICT: overlap/contact is measured on the world-positioned meshes (vertex containment + nearest-vertex gap). For fasteners (bolt through a hole, nut on a thread) this overlap is EXPECTED and is NOT asserted to be a fault — it is a signal an engineer reads.

Service world — future SUGGESTION

NOT BUILT HERE (future SUGGESTION tier): inferring a part's service world (exterior/internal) from its role/position would be a labelled suggestion the user confirms, never a fabricated fact.
